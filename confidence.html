How Do I Become Confident when Public Speaking?

Here are tips to look and sound confident while public speaking!

I don't feel confident giving speeches at all. How do I suddenly become confident?

No one can go from shy one day to confident the next. There is a step in between:
to sound and look confident and to not sound and look shy. Here are some ways to achieve that!

Steps to Gaining Confidence



Step 1: Not Looking Scared

Improve your posture, use more open-body language, and give better eye contact.


Step 2: Not Sounding Scared

Speak louder, end your sentences on a lower pitch, and use pauses when you speak. In addition, be wary of your speaking rate. Don't talk too fast or slow, it will make you sound panicked or unsure.


Step 3: Adopt a Curious Mindset

Instead of focusing on yourself, focus on the audience you are talking about. The moment you shift your attention away from yourself and to the audience, you'll stop being self-conscious and eliminate the awkward feeling.


Step 4: Wear Clothes You're Comfortable In

A lot of the times, we judge ourselves based on what we wear. Therefore, wear clothes that make you feel confident while speaking. This is called enclothed cognition. The research behind enclothed cognition suggests that its not so much about what we wear, but what we think about what we wear.


Step 5: Master Your Breathing

Oftentimes, normal (chest) breathing doesn't supply enough oxygen for a long speech, which can lead to shortness of breath. This can make you found nervous or jittery. To fix this, make sure to take deep breaths at a controlled pace when you take a pause in your speech. Additionally, make sure to take a strong breath before you start speaking.


Step 6: Say Your Speech Out Loud

Saying your speech out loud will help you be more familiar with the flow of the words, which is important in order to reduce stuttering. Stuttering makes you sound nervous, and to avoid that you must familiarize yourself with the pronounciation of the words. This will also help you ingrain your speech in your head more so you don't have to look down at your notecards 24/7.


Step 7: Sound Happy

Whatever emotion you sound actually induces the same emotion in the audience. Studies have shown that there is a direct correlation between how our voices sound and how we feel. Thus, sounding happier will make the audience happier to listen to you as well.

Additionally, to reduce nervousness, pretend you're having a conversation with a specific audience member. Speak as if you're having a converation with a friend, which will make you more relaxed and allow the audience to warm up to you. It will also give the audience an impression of closeness and friendship, which will make them want to listen to you more.


Step 8: Channel Your Fear

Use your nervous energy and channel it into passion. When you're speaking and you feel your heart racing or your palms sweating, reframe the 'nervousness' emotion as excitement. Then, you'll sound more passionate and into what you're speaking about.
