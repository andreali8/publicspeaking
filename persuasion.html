Persuasion Tips

Here are some persuasion tips to quickly enhance your persuasive speaking ability.

Ethos, Pathos, Logos

To be a successful speaker, one needs to utilize Ethos, Pathos, and Logos, which are

Ethos refers to the appeal to credibility. The speaker can communicate their authority over the subject by using credible sources, having proper credentials, and choosing the appropriate language (using no slang and correct pronounciation of words).

Pathos refers to the appeal to emotion. It means to persuade an audience by purposefully evoking the audience's emotions. Speakers can do this by telling impactful stories, making deliberate word choices, and using the correct tones that evoke emotion.

Logos refers to the appeal to logic. To use logos, the speaker needs to make clear, logical connections between ideas, using things like facts or statistics. There should be no fallacies, or assumptions, in the logical reasoning the speaking is making.

Examples of Ethos, Logos, and Pathos


Imagine this: a small dog sits in a dark, cold garage. His hair is matted and dirty; he is skinny and weak from going days without food. There is no water for him to drink, no person to give him love and no blanket to keep him warm at night. While this might be a hard scenario to imagine, it is not an uncommon one in America today. According to the Humane Society of the United States, nearly 1,000,000 animals are abused or die from abuse every year. As a veterinarian with 30 years of experience, I have seen how even one incident of abuse can affect an animal for the rest of its life. As a society, we need to be more aware of this terrible problem and address this issue before it gets worse.

ANSWERS

  • As a veterinarian with 30 years of experience, I have seen how even one incident of abuse can affect an animal for the rest of its life.

    This is ethos because it is appealing to the author's credibility, which is established when the author states their own experiences and occupation. The author has a lot of experience dealing with mistreated animals, and thus is reliable.

  • According to the Humane Society of the United States, nearly 1,000,000 animals are abused or die from abuse every year.

    This is logos because the author is using a reliable resource as a statistic to appeal to our intellect.

  • There is no water for him to drink, no person to give him love and no blanket to keep him warm at night.

    This is pathos because the author is using the sad story of the dog to evoke an emotional reaction from the reader. The author is trying to make the reader feel sadness and pity for the mistreated animal.
